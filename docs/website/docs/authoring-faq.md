# Frequently Asked Questions and Community Norms for Authorship

## Purpose and Scope

This guide responds to frequently asked questions about Gala authorship and also describes our community norms, which have been established through careful work with our collaborators. Conversations around some of these issues are very much ongoing, and we invite you to contribute your own questions and concerns at hello@learngala.com.

## General Questions

### What is a module? What is a case study?

The Gala platform started as a way to showcase teaching case studies, which are narrative-based teaching tools focusing on a specific time and place. These example stories are a way of learning about broader issues and concepts while remaining grounded. You can learn more about them in our [Planning a Case Guide](./advanced-planning-your-case#what-is-a-case-study).

Over time, authors began to create learning materials on Gala that went beyond this definition and some of our user communities prefer the term “learning module” to describe what they create. Learning modules are often described as self-contained units of instruction. Learners are typically said to engage with a module on their own in a structured way, though Gala modules are implemented in many different ways.

We use the two terms interchangeably. 

:::tip 
The easiest way to get a feel for what a Gala module looks like is to skim through a few on your own.
:::

### Who can author a module or case study on Gala?

Anyone is welcome to create a module, so long as:

1. The module is intended for educational purposes.
2. You respect the Gala [Terms of Service](../policies) and the community norms described in this document.

## Openness

### What does it mean that Gala is an “open platform”?

We consider Gala to be “open” in three different but related senses:
Modules you find or author on Gala are Open Educational Resources, bearing Creative Commons licenses that allow others to redistribute and remix them.
Modules are [Open Access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_access), meaning that readers have free and immediate access to them online.
The Gala platform is developed as an [open source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source) web application, meaning that the code can be used and redistributed by anyone. (see our code on GitHub)

### How is my work on Gala licensed?

Gala offers several [Creative Commons (CC)](https://creativecommons.org/cc-licenses/) licensing options for authors. Creative Commons licenses allow you to retain ownership of the work while granting certain rights to the public to use or modify it.
 When creating a module, authors must select one of the following three licensing options:

1. **Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International** [(CC BY-NC-SA 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/4.0/deed.en) — This license allows others to share and adapt a module for non-commercial purposes, as long as they give proper credit. Any adapted versions must carry the same license.

2. **Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International** [(CC BY-NC-ND 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-nd/4.0/deed.en) — This license is similar to CC BY-NC-SA but does not permit others to adapt or repurpose your module.

3. **Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International** [(CC BY-SA 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/deed.en) — This license allows sharing and adaptation, even for commercial uses, as long as attribution is preserved and the new work carries the same license.

:::note
Some modules created before the adoption of CC Licensing may have _All Rights Reserved_ licensing. Please see the [Module Licensing guide](./general-licensing) for more information.
:::

:::info

####
Open Educational Resources have been framed in terms of “Five Rs,” which stand for your rights with respect to a resource:

- **Retain** — The right to make, own, and control copies of the content. 
- **Revise** — The right to edit, adapt, and modify your copy of the resource (e.g., translate into another language). On Gala, modules can be deployed to a specific learning group and discussed within a relevant context.
- **Remix** — The ability to combine your original or revised copy with other existing material to create something new. 
- **Reuse** — The ability to use your original, revised, or remixed copy publicly. C
- **Redistribute** — The ability to share copies of your original, revised, or remixed resource with others. Authors are not restricted in where they can post or share their module.

_Adapted from [Defining the "Open" in Open Content and Open Educational Resources](https://opencontent.org/definition/) by David Wiley_

:::

## Authorship

### Who qualifies as an author?

An author is someone who contributes substantially to the development of the module, including but not limited to conception, design, research, writing, drafting, and revising it critically for intellectual content. Different libraries may have different norms and thresholds for what constitutes authorship. There is no limit to the number of authors who may be included and Gala does not enforce any particular significance to author order. 

### How can I recognize contributors who are not authors?

Authors have the opportunity to recognize supporters and contributors through acknowledgements, which appear on the overview page under the authors. It is common to acknowledge funders, reviewers, and specific programs or groups. However, it is appropriate to recognize any persons or organizations that you feel supported the module without meeting the threshold of authorship.

### Can I or someone else make changes or updates to a module that I have published?

Yes! Edits and updates are highly encouraged, but be mindful that others may be using your published module. If you are planning to make major changes (changing the target audience, for example), an adaptation may be more appropriate.

:::note

Authors are also encouraged to review and critically consider feedback provided from the community and make updates as deemed necessary. This means that you may be contacted about your module by someone interested in contributing to it or building off of it. As a member of the Gala community, we ask you to approach these potential partnerships with generosity and curiosity, understanding that an original version of your work can be preserved as the content evolves.
:::

### How do I adapt a module on Gala?

Most Gala modules carry licenses that allow others to “remix” or adapt them and, if they do, you are free to do so.

We are a small community and are still working on infrastructure that allows us to manage adaptations in ways that keep attribution clear and don’t clutter up search results. This means that we handle a lot of this manually for the time being. If you’re interested in adapting a module on Gala, get in contact with us at hello@learngala.com and we can help you reach out to the module author or library manager. You can also contact the module author directly.

### Can I republish my Gala module somewhere else? What about republishing something on Gala that was published elsewhere?

Some publishers restrict what you can do with material that you publish with them, so be sure to check on their rules and licensing. Gala modules are published with Creative Commons licenses that permit reuse elsewhere as long as proper attribution is maintained, so you can republish your modules anywhere you choose. If your module is based on work published elsewhere, include a link to the original work.

## Content Quality & Moderation

### How is content moderated on Gala?

Each library is responsible for setting and enforcing their own standards — library managers ensure that content aligns with library norms and Gala's policies. Since case studies are not held to strict peer review standards, Gala trusts and expects authors to perform sufficient research, including responses to existing or expected critical feedback.

:::note
The Gala team may reach out to you with opportunities to improve the quality of your module or to make updates that keep it current.
:::

### Does Gala ever remove content from the platform?

The Gala team has the right (and responsibility) to remove content if it violates policy and could be harmful to other users. The Gala team typically would reach out to authors before removal to discuss opportunities for amendment.

## AI Use

### Can I use AI when creating a Gala module?

AI and its role in how we create, teach, and learn, is subject to much debate and norms are rapidly evolving. Use of AI on Gala is not discouraged, but it should be used with care.

Our general guidelines are:

- **Publish only “human-made” works** – The use of AI as a tool to render specific results is permitted, but the product must be the result of human creativity, intellect, and control over the work's expression, development, and completion. (This follows precedent created by the Federal Register, which says that copyright can be granted only to content created by humans; the term "author" excludes non-human entities.)
- **Avoid “slop”** – The explosion of AI-generated content online has trained many readers to treat LLM writing patterns and AI-generated media with suspicion, often perceiving it as low quality, lazy, or potentially misleading. Make sure that you are publishing a module that readers will feel is worth their time to engage with.
- **Disclose and attribute AI use** – Be honest and forthright about AI use in your module.
- **Check for quality and infringement** – LLMs and other AI tools may “hallucinate” and make mistakes, including creating citations that don’t exist. These tools may, in some cases, generate work that is derivative of others without providing proper attribution (or work that may infringe on the intellectual property of others. Verify the accuracy and quality of any outputs.

### How should I disclose AI use in my module?

The method of citation varies depending on the use:

- **Acknowledgements** – e.g., _"We would like to acknowledge our use of ChatGPT, which was used to do xyz for this work."_
- **In-text citations** (using the citation function) – written directly in the text, e.g., _"This language translation was completed with the assistance of Anthropic Claude Sonnet 4.7. Outputs were checked for accuracy and amended when necessary by x, who is proficient in y language."_
- **Multimedia** (using the edgenote attribution function) – Write _“Generated by [model name]."_ in the attribution box.

### Will my content be used to train AI models?

By contributing to Gala, users join an international and interdisciplinary community where all content is open access. Users should be aware that–like all content freely available on the internet–modules created on Gala may be "scraped" by third parties to train AI models and interpreted in ways that the author may not intend. Per our privacy policy, Gala will not “sell or rent your personal data.”
